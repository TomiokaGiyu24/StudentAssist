# Physics Derivations Diagram Prompts

This file contains the diagram descriptions for all physics derivations, organized by chapter. These descriptions can be used as prompts for generating or verifying the diagrams.

## Chapter 1: Electric Charges and Fields

| ID | Title | Diagram Description |
|---|---|---|


| ch1-01 | Electric Field due to an Electric Dipole on the Axial Line | Horizontal axis.
 Dipole placed on x-axis: charge -q at A and +q at B separated by distance 2a. Center of dipole at Origin O. Point P is on the right side of +q at distance r from O. Vector E_+q at P points to the right (away). Vector E_-q at P points to the left (towards). Since P is closer to +q, vector E_+q is drawn longer than E_-q. Dipole moment p points from -q to +q. |

| ch1-02 | Electric Field due to an Electric Dipole on the Equatorial Plane | 
Dipole -q at A and +q at B on horizontal line. O is midpoint. P is a point on perpendicular bisector at distance r from O. Triangle PAB is isosceles. Distance AP = BP = sqrt(r^2 + a^2). Vector E_+q at P points away from B. Vector E_-q at P points towards A. Resolve both vectors: Vertical components (E sin theta) cancel. Horizontal components (E cos theta) add up pointing antiparallel to dipole moment p. |


| ch1-03 | Torque on an Electric Dipole in a Uniform Electric Field | Uniform electric field lines (horizontal arrows to the right). 
Dipole placed at angle theta to field. Force on +q is qE (along field). Force on -q is -qE (opposite to field). Perpendicular distance between lines of action of forces is labeled as 2a sin(theta). |


| ch1-04 | Electric Field due to an Infinitely Long Straight Charged Wire (Gauss's Law) | Vertical thin infinite line of positive charge with linear density lambda. Cylindrical Gaussian surface of radius r and length l coaxial with the wire. Electric field vectors E pointing radially outward. Area vector dS on curved surface is parallel to E. Area vector dS on flat circular ends is perpendicular to E. |


| ch1-05 | Electric Field due to a Uniformly Charged Infinite Plane Sheet | Vertical plane sheet with positive charge density sigma. Gaussian surface is a cylindrical 'pillbox' piercing the sheet. Flat circular faces of area A are on both sides of the sheet at distance r. Electric field vectors point outward from the sheet on both sides (parallel to area vectors of flat faces). |


| ch1-06 | Electric Field due to a Uniformly Charged Thin Spherical Shell | Thin spherical shell of radius R with charge +q. Two Gaussian spheres shown: one inside (radius r < R) and one outside (radius r > R). Electric field vectors shown radially outward for the outside case. |


| ch1-07 | Coulomb's Law in Vector Form | Two point charges q1 and q2 in space with position vectors r1 and r2. Vector r21 points from q1 to q2. Force F21 acts on q2. Force F12 acts on q1. |
