=== CHAPTER 1: MANAGEMENT OF SPORTING EVENTS ===

Diagram name - Knock-out Fixture (11 Teams)
Used for - Visualizing the flow of matches and bye placement
Description - A vertical list of numbers 1 to 11. A horizontal line separates Upper Half (1-6) and Lower Half (7-11). Byes (16-11=5) are marked next to: Team 11 (B1), Team 1 (B2), Team 7 (B3), Team 6 (B4), Team 10 (B5). Round 1 matches: Team 2 vs 3, Team 4 vs 5, Team 8 vs 9. Round 2: Team 1 plays winner of (2v3), Team 6 plays winner of (4v5), etc. Arrows connect winners moving rightward to Semis and Final.

---

Diagram name - Staircase Method (5 Teams)
Used for - League fixture representation
Description - Resembles a staircase descending from left to right. Step 1: 1-2, 1-3, 1-4, 1-5. Step 2: 2-3, 2-4, 2-5. Step 3: 3-4, 3-5. Step 4: 4-5. Each pair represents a match. No byes used here.


=== CHAPTER 2: CHILDREN AND WOMEN IN SPORTS ===

Diagram name - Spinal Deformities (Stick Figures)
Used for - Visualizing Kyphosis, Lordosis, Scoliosis
Description - Three side-profile stick figures. Kyphosis: Spine curves significantly outwards at the top (hump). Lordosis: Spine curves deeply inwards at the lower back (belly out). Scoliosis: (Back view) Spine curves sideways like an 'S'.

---

Diagram name - Footprint Comparison
Used for - Flat Foot explanation
Description - Two footprint outlines. Normal Foot: Has a curve/gap on the inner side. Flat Foot: The entire sole imprint is visible with no curve/gap.


=== CHAPTER 3: YOGA AS PREVENTIVE MEASURE ===

Diagram name - Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)
Used for - Diabetes, Back Pain, Asthma
Description - A stick figure lying on its stomach. The legs are flat on the ground. The upper body (torso) is raised in a curve, supported by arms pushing against the ground. Head is tilted back looking up. Resembles a snake lifting its head.

---

Diagram name - Trikonasana (Triangle Pose)
Used for - Obesity
Description - A standing stick figure with legs wide apart. The torso is bent sideways. One arm reaches down to touch the foot, the other arm extends straight up towards the sky. The arms form a straight vertical line.

---

Diagram name - Pavanmuktasana (Wind-Relieving Pose)
Used for - Gas, Diabetes
Description - A stick figure lying on its back. The knees are bent and pulled close to the chest. The arms are wrapped around the shins. The head is lifted slightly to touch the knees.

=== CHAPTER 6: TEST & MEASUREMENT ===

Diagram name - Sit and Reach Test
Used for - Explaining Flexibility Test
Description - A student sitting on the floor with legs stretched out straight. Feet are placed flat against a testing box. The student reaches forward with both hands to touch the box/scale. Measures flexibility of hamstrings and lower back.

---

Diagram name - Flamingo Balance Test
Used for - Static Balance explanation
Description - A student standing on one leg on a narrow beam. The other leg is bent at the knee and held by the hand. The free hand tries to maintain balance. The examiner times how long balance is held.

---

Diagram name - Chair Stand Test
Used for - Senior Citizen Test
Description - An elderly person sitting on a chair with arms crossed against the chest. The diagram shows two phases: 'Sitting' and 'Standing up' fully, then returning to sit. Repeated for 30 seconds.


=== CHAPTER 7: PHYSIOLOGY & INJURIES ===

Diagram name - Greenstick Fracture
Used for - Types of Fractures
Description - A long bone (like forearm) that is bent like a green twig. One side of the bone is broken/splintered, but the other side is intact (bent).

---

Diagram name - Comminuted Fracture
Used for - Types of Fractures
Description - A bone where the fracture site has shattered into 3 or more small bony fragments/pieces.

---

Diagram name - Sports Injury Classification Flowchart
Used for - Classification questions
Description - A flowchart. Top: 'Sports Injuries'. Branch 1: 'Soft Tissue' (Sprain, Strain, Contusion, Abrasion). Branch 2: 'Hard Tissue'. Sub-branch A: 'Joint Injuries' (Dislocation). Sub-branch B: 'Bone Injuries' (Fractures).

Diagram name - Isometric vs Isotonic Exercises
Used for - Differentiation question
Description - Isometric: Person pushing against a solid wall (No movement). Isotonic: Person lifting a dumbbell (Arm bends, muscle bulges).

---

Diagram name - Interval Training Heart Rate Graph
Used for - Interval Method explanation
Description - A saw-tooth graph. Vertical axis = Heart Rate. Horizontal axis = Time. The line goes UP (Work phase, HR rises to 180) then DOWN (Recovery phase, HR drops to 120), repeated multiple times.
